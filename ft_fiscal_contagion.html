The Fiscal Contagion: AI Agency and the Collapse of Labor Revenues

As AI task horizons grow logarithmically, Belgium faces a structural collapse of its wage-dependent tax base — and a narrow window to engineer a post-labor equilibrium

The exponential trajectory of artificial intelligence has breached a critical economic threshold. Leveraging data from the Model Evaluation and Threat Research (METR) organization, we observe that AI capabilities are no longer measured by static benchmarks, but by the "Task Horizon"—the duration for which an AI can autonomously plan and execute complex tasks with a 50% success rate. This metric is growing logarithmically. While models like ChatGPT handled micro-tasks, by 2030, models will seamlessly handle "1-Month Tasks," effectively approaching AGI and operating as fully autonomous synthetic employees.

The implications for the Belgian labor market are profound and deeply destabilizing. Belgium's economy relies heavily on white-collar cognitive labor. As AI crosses the 1-Day task threshold in 2027, structural unemployment will violently decouple from its 5% baseline, rocketing past the 15% Job Guarantee trigger and peaking at an apocalyptic 35% around 2032.

This job shock triggers an immediate fiscal contagion. Currently, the Belgian state relies massively on the Personal Income Tax (IPP) and social contributions (ONSS). As human labor is systematically substituted by near-zero marginal cost AI inference, this labor-tax base will structurally collapse. Total Tax-to-GDP is projected to plummet from a robust 43% down to a fiscally insolvent 28% at the 2032 crisis point, sparking a lethal Revenue-Debt Trap as nominal obligations remain fixed while the tax base evaporates.

To survive this valley of death, policymakers must engineer an "Elegant Equilibrium." This perfect scenario embraces the aggressive deflationary nature of AI to fund a localized Universal Basic Income, counterbalancing the fiat inflation required to keep the unemployed populace afloat. More critically, the state must transition from a wage-based economy to a property-based macroeconomy. By utilizing aggressive government matching funds and a 0.5% wealth circulation tax on ultra-high accounts, displaced laborers can be transitioned into sovereign owners of the very capital replacing them. Finally, the implementation of a sweeping "Compute Tax" in 2035 will restabilize the fiscal state at a 30% GDP ratio, ushering in an era of post-labor hyperabundance.
